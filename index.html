My Engineering Philosophy: The Two Sons Rule

"In my development journey, I believe in Proportional Security. For ShadowPad, I gave the 'Elder Son' (the permanent Vault) a heavy PBKDF2 'Big Stick' for long-term armor. For the 'Younger Son' (the Live Room), I used lightweight ephemeral keys to keep the speed fast. This balance of Security vs. Usability is how I approach every system I build."

ShadowPad: End-to-End Encrypted Collaborative Workspace

The Core: A zero-knowledge notepad that ensures total user privacy through a custom-built ShadowRatchet encryption protocol.

Technical Achievement: Engineered a high-concurrency environment using Socket.io, managing up to 100 synchronized users with a 250ms debounced relay system to optimize server performance on a $0 budget.

The Security: Implemented AES-256-GCM encryption where keys never leave the client’s browser, providing Forward Secrecy—meaning even a future server breach cannot decrypt past conversations.

🛡️ Advanced Cryptography & Real-Time Systems

  • Protocol Design: Developed a multi-user Symmetric Ratchet system based on the Signal Protocol (X3DH/Double Ratchet principles).
  • Key Hardening: Utilized PBKDF2 with 100,000 iterations for secure key derivation, effectively neutralizing brute-force dictionary attacks.
  • Traffic Engineering: Reduced server-side overhead by 90% through Temporal Message Aggregation, allowing complex cryptographic sync without lagging the UI.
